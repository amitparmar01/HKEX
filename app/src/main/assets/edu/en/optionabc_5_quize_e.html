1. As the implied volatility of a put option increases, the premium of this put option will generally

    1. increase 
    2. decrease 
    3. remain the same 
    4. can't be determined 
    The correct answer is "A"

    Explanation

    The premium of both put and call options will increase when the volatility of these options increases. The greater the volatility, the greater the probability that the options will become in-the-money.

  2. As the underlying stock price increases, keeping other factors constant, the premium of a put option

    1. will generally increase 
    2. will generally decrease 
    3. will remain the same 
    4. can't be determined 
    The correct answer is "B"

    Explanation

    The premium of an option changes as the market price of the underlying security moves. If the stock price increases the premium of a put option will decrease as its intrinsic value decreases.

  3. A call is out-of-the-money when the market price of the underlying stock is

    1. equal to the strike price 
    2. above the strike price 
    3. below the strike price 
    4. equal to the strike price plus or minus the premium 
    The correct answer is "C"

    Explanation

    By definition, a call is out-of-the-money when the market price of the underlying stock is below the strike price, and there is no intrinsic value in the premium.

  4. XYZ stock is now trading at $70. XYZ APR $60 Put is trading at a premium of $1.5. What is the intrinsic value of these puts?

    1. $0 
    2. $1.5 
    3. $10 
    4. can't be determined 
    The correct answer is "A"

    Explanation

    Intrinsic value is the difference between the option's strike price and the price of the underlying stock. However, only in-the-money options would have intrinsic value. Here the XYZ Puts are out-of-the-money and they have no intrinsic value.

  5. Which of the following strategies can be applied when you would like to benefit in a steady market?

    I. long call

    II. long put

    III. short call

    IV. short put

    1. I and II 
    2. I and IV  
    3. II and III 
    4. III and IV 
    The correct answer is "D"

    Explanation

    In a steady market, the premium of an option will reduce over time. This is an expense for those who are long in both calls and puts, and a source of income for those who are short.

  6. Time decay of option value is favorable to

    1. option holders 
    2. option writers 
    3. market makers 
    4. all of the above 
    The correct answer is "B"

    Explanation

    Time decay is unfavorable to the option holder, but favorable to the option writer. The less time remaining, the smaller the chance that the option will expire in-the-money.
