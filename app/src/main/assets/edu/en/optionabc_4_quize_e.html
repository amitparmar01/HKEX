1. Which of the following investors will benefit in a bearish market?

    II. Holders of a call

    II. Holders of a put

    III. Writers of a call

    IV. Writers of a put

    1. I and II 
    2. I and III 
    3. II and III 
    4. III and IV 
    The correct answer is "C"

    Explanation

    Holders of puts and writers of calls are bearish as they will benefit if the underlying stock price drops. Holders of puts may exercise the right to sell the stock at the relatively higher strike price and buy back the stocks to profit. Writers of calls will gain the premium as the holders will give up the right to exercise the call.

  2. Which option strategy best suits a bearish view?

    1. Buy call option 
    2. Sell call option 
    3. Buy put option 
    4. Sell put option 
    The correct answer is "C"

    Explanation

    The purchase of a put option reflects a view that the share price will fall.

  3. You cannot enjoy the benefit of a rise in share price if you buy put option to protect your shares from a fall in price.

    1. True 
    2. False 
    The correct answer is "B"

    Explanation

    Buying put options means you can protect your shares from a fall in price. You still maintain exposure to a rise in the share price.
